<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Coin Spending Order">

The safest spending order is simple: fix survival first, improve unit quality second, and buy zones only after the run feels controlled. That order keeps your currency from getting trapped in the wrong upgrade at the wrong time.

When you feel stuck, ask one question: **what stopped the last run?** If the answer is damage, spend on damage. If the answer is coverage, strengthen placement or support. If the answer is weak pulls, save for a better roll window.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stabilize the current wave">
    Make sure zombies are not slipping through your line. Do not treat any currency as spare until the defense can clear the lane without panic spending.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Fix the real bottleneck">
    Spend on the problem that actually caused the leak. Damage problems and coverage problems are different, so avoid blanket spending.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Roll during a good luck window">
    Use friend luck and the creator group bonus before a long rolling session. That is the cleanest time to convert saved income into stronger units.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Buy zones after stability">
    Expand only when your current setup can handle the pressure. A new zone should improve progress, not force emergency repairs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Spending Choice | Best Time | Skip It When |
|---|---|---|
| Defense upgrade | Zombies survive too long | The issue is bad placement, not weak damage |
| Unit roll | You have saved income and luck is active | Your current wave is already failing |
| Zone purchase | The defense is stable and income is healthy | The zone would drain your entire budget |
| Saving coins | You have not identified the bottleneck | You already know exactly what must improve |

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Overbuy">
A new zone can look like progress, but if it exposes a weak defense, you will spend even more coins fixing the mistake. Delay expansion until the current loop is clean.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Roll, Upgrade, or Zone?">

Once you understand the economy, the real question becomes where your coins should go next. The answer changes based on the run state, but the logic stays the same: choose the purchase that removes the biggest blocker.

Use **rolls** when stronger units would clearly improve your field. Use **upgrades** when the current setup is already good but not quite strong enough. Use **zones** when the existing defense is stable and you want more progression room.

| Option | Best For | Main Risk | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roll | Chasing stronger units | Low-value pulls | Best when your defense can already survive |
| Upgrade | Fixing a weak lane or damage gap | Spending too early | Best first fix when waves are leaking |
| Zone | Pushing progression | Overextending your budget | Best only after stability is proven |
| Save | Waiting for a better decision | Slower short-term growth | Smart when the bottleneck is unclear |

<Section icon={Map} title="A Safer Farming Route for More Coins">

The best farming route is a repeatable loop, not a one-time burst. Start by collecting offline income, then roll during your strongest luck setup, then spend only after the defense proves it can hold. That rhythm keeps your economy from stalling between sessions.

This approach works because Roll to Defend has a built-in pacing system: waves, zone pressure, roll value, and offline gain all feed into each other. When one part is weak, the whole run feels slower. When one part is strong, every coin stretches further.

| Farming Phase | Action | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Start | Collect offline income | Begin with full spending power |
| Mid | Roll after luck bonuses are active | Improve unit quality efficiently |
| Late | Reinvest into the weakest point | Keep the run from collapsing |
| Push | Buy zones only if the defense stays stable | Convert strength into progression |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Open with offline income">
    Claim your returned value first. That gives you the cleanest budget for the rest of the session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check the last failure point">
    Decide whether the last problem was damage, coverage, or unit quality. That tells you where your coins belong.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Activate your luck setup">
    Use friends and the D:/Drive group before a long rolling session so your currency goes into better pulls.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Spend only after stability">
    If the defense is holding, roll or expand. If it is failing, fix the failure first.
  </Step>
</Steps>
