Luck matters, but it should never replace good spending. Use income to stabilize the run first, then convert the leftover value into rolls, and only then think about a new zone. That order keeps you from widening your problems while you are still weak.

<Callout type="info" title="Core Rule">
Spend on the bottleneck that ended your last run. If you do not know the bottleneck, pause and identify it before you buy anything else.
</Callout>

| Situation | Best spend | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Zombies reach the base | Core damage | You need stronger clearing power |
| Lanes leak despite good damage | Coverage or placement | Your board needs better path control |
| Defense feels stable | More rolls | Better pulls become the next upgrade |

A useful editorial rule is simple: buy safety before ambition. If your strongest unit is not carrying the board, another zone usually makes the pressure worse. If the board is stable, more rolls and a clean luck stack can deliver the next real upgrade.

| Priority | Spend on | Delay when |

Use the official game page as your anchor, then treat every upgrade as part of a loop: defend, collect, roll, and expand. That loop is stronger than chasing luck alone.
