<Section icon={Shield} title="The First Session Loop">

Your first session should feel repetitive in a good way. Roll a few times, place what you get, test the defense, then spend toward the weakest link. That keeps you from burning resources on upgrades that look exciting but do not solve the actual problem.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Roll for a usable base">
    Start by rolling until you have enough defenders to stop the earliest waves from slipping through.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Place units with purpose">
    Put units where they can hit enemies for the longest time, not where they only look tidy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Watch the leak point">
    Find the lane or section where zombies survive the longest, then reinforce that spot first.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reinvest income carefully">
    Use income to support the weakest part of the run before you chase a bigger roll session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Expand only after stability">
    Buy the next zone once the current defense clears without panic spending.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Early Mistakes">
Do not buy a new zone just because it is available. If your current wave is still shaky, expansion usually makes the next problem larger.
</Callout>

| Do | Avoid | Result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Place your best pull first | Saving strong units for later | Better early wave control |
| Reinforce the leak point | Spreading damage evenly everywhere | Faster wave cleanup |
| Use income on the bottleneck | Buying random upgrades | Cleaner progression |
| Expand after stable clears | Rushing zones during pressure | Less resource waste |

A good session feels boring in the best way. You should always know what the next purchase is solving. If you cannot name the problem, wait, collect more income, and fix the weakest layer first.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Upgrades, Income, and Spending Order">

Upgrades work best when they support a clear purpose. Think of them as a tool for removing friction. If zombies are reaching the base, improve survival. If waves are controlled, improve roll quality or future income. That is the difference between progress and busywork.

<Callout type="info" title="Spend for the Bottleneck">
The right upgrade is the one that removes the exact problem that ended your last run.
</Callout>

| Upgrade Focus | Priority | Buy When | Delay When |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Core damage | High | Enemies survive too long | Placement is the real issue |
| Coverage | High | Zombies slip past gaps | Your main unit is too weak |
| Roll quality setup | Medium | You are preparing a long session | Current waves are already failing |
| Duplicate handling | Medium | A duplicate clearly improves the field | Replacing it opens a lane |
| Zone-ready investment | Medium | Waves clear cleanly | Defense still feels unstable |
| Offline income spend | Flexible | You return with saved earnings | You have not identified the bottleneck |

The cleanest spending order is usually survival first, roll quality second, and expansion third. That order keeps your run from collapsing under its own ambition. Income is strongest when it is treated like a repair budget, not a shopping spree.

| Income Source | Best Use | Best Timing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wave clears | Fund the next roll | After stable defense |
| Saved earnings | Patch weak spots | Between sessions |
| Offline income | Restart momentum | On login |
| Group or friend bonuses | Improve roll value | Before long sessions |

If your income feels small, the answer is usually not “grind harder.” The answer is to stop wasting it on purchases that do not solve the current wave problem.

</Section>
