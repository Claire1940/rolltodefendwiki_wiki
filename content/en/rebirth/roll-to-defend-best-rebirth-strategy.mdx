<Section icon={Map} title="Follow the Best Progression Order">

Once your first defense is stable, the next step is simple: identify the bottleneck and spend on that, not on whatever looks exciting. In Roll to Defend, that usually means choosing between stronger rolls, better coverage, or a safer zone purchase. The order matters because every bad spend delays the next strong wave of progress.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Collect Your Current Value">
    Start by collecting offline income and checking what failed in your last run. If the last loss came from leaks, the problem is defense. If the run felt weak, the problem is usually unit quality or placement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Roll With a Goal">
    Roll only after you know what you are trying to improve. A random roll session wastes value when you already know your weak point.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Place the Best Unit First">
    Put your strongest pull on the map before filling in the rest. Strong units should get the best path coverage and the longest uptime.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Clear Waves Before Expansion">
    Let the current zone prove itself. If the defense is still shaky, buying the next zone usually makes the problem louder.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Expand Only With Backup Income">
    Buy the new zone when you still have enough money left to react. Expansion should support progress, not drain your ability to survive.
  </Step>
</Steps>
