<Section icon={Target} title="Upgrade Path: Where to Spend Income First">

Income in Roll to Defend should go to the weakest point that actually caused your last leak. If zombies are reaching the base, fix damage or coverage before you chase more rolls or a new zone.

<Callout type="warning" title="Spend Order">
If the current wave is unstable, upgrades come before expansion. Buying a zone too early can stretch your setup past its limit.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the bottleneck">
    Decide whether the run failed because of damage, coverage, roll quality, or bad zone timing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Fix the active defense">
    Spend on the unit or placement that already removes the most zombies, then patch the lane that leaks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Roll with purpose">
    Save income for a focused roll session after your defense is stable and your luck setup is active.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Expand only after stability">
    Buy the next zone when the current setup clears cleanly and you still have enough income to react.
  </Step>
</Steps>

|---|---|---|---|
| Core damage | High | Zombies survive too long | Leaks are caused by bad placement |
| Coverage | High | Lanes slip through gaps | Main damage is still too weak |
| Roll quality setup | Medium | You have saved income and luck bonuses active | The current wave is already failing |
| Duplicate handling | Medium | The new copy clearly improves the field | It removes useful coverage |
| Zone-ready spending | Medium | Waves clear without panic spending | Expansion would make the next wave harder |

A practical rule: fix survival first, then improve the economy loop. That keeps your strongest pulls useful instead of letting them sit behind a bad spending pattern.

<Section icon={Map} title="Practical Unit Rules for Faster Progress">

The best unit choice depends on the stage of your run. Early on, you want a reliable holder. Mid-game, you want a real carry. Later, you want the pull that keeps new zones from collapsing the moment you unlock them.

<Callout type="tip" title="Stage Rule">
A unit that looks average on paper can still be your best choice if it prevents leaks in the exact spot that matters.
</Callout>

| Game Stage | What to Prioritize | What to Avoid | Best Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early game | Path coverage, easy placement | Overinvesting in flashy pulls | Clean first waves |
| Mid game | Strongest visible rarity | Filling the board with weak units | Stable defense core |
| Zone push | Stability and lane control | Buying a zone too soon | Safer expansion |
| Return session | Fixing old bottlenecks | Spending all income on rolls | Faster recovery |
| Late game | Long-term keep value | Replacing good units too early | Better run efficiency |

| Unit Type | Role | Keep or Replace |
|---|---|---|
| Divine pull | Main carry | Keep |
| Highest-rarity current pull | Reliable anchor | Keep |
| Early damage pull | Temporary support | Replace later |
| Outclassed filler | Empty-slot coverage | Replace |
| Zone-stabilizing pull | Expansion support | Keep if it protects the run |

If you want a clean decision rule, use this: keep the unit that improves the worst part of your defense, not the one that merely looks rarer in isolation.
