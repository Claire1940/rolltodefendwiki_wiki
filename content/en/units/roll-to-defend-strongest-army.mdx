<Section icon={Swords} title="How to rank units without wasting income">

If you want the strongest army, rank units by what they do on the field, not by how flashy the pull looks. A lower-rarity unit that covers a lane well can outperform a stronger unit that sits in the wrong spot.

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Rank by Rarity Alone">
A rare pull is exciting, but a rare pull that fails to cover the path is still a weak investment for the current run.
</Callout>

| Ranking factor | Why it matters | Good sign |
|---|---|---|
| Damage output | Clears waves faster | Enemies disappear before reaching pressure points |
| Path coverage | Stops leaks across more of the map | The unit reaches a long lane or key choke |
| Cost efficiency | Protects your income | The unit gives strong value for the roll or upgrade price |
| Scaling | Keeps value in later zones | The unit still matters after the first few waves |
| Stability | Reduces babysitting | The defense works without constant manual fixes |

| Keep or replace? | Keep it | Replace it |
|---|---|---|
| First solid pull | Yes, if it anchors your setup | Only if a clearly stronger clear unit appears |
| Duplicate filler | Maybe, if it still helps coverage | If it reduces lane control or slows progress |
| Rare pull | Yes, almost always | Never swap it for a weaker option |
| Early starter | Temporary | As soon as a better defender is online |

<Section icon={Target} title="Use a step-by-step build order">

The safest way to grow is to follow a repeatable loop. Set your first defender, stabilize your waves, then reinvest into the part of the setup that is actually slowing you down.

<Callout type="success" title="Best Build Order">
A stable army usually grows in this order: **core defender first**, **coverage second**, **upgrades third**, **zones last**.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure one reliable attacker">
    Roll until you have one unit that can carry early waves. Your first goal is not perfection; it is survival plus momentum.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Place for coverage">
    Put the unit where it can hit the longest stretch of path or the most dangerous choke. A good unit in the wrong spot still underperforms.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spend on the bottleneck">
    If zombies survive too long, buy damage. If they leak around the lane, buy coverage or reposition support. Fix the real problem first.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Buy zones after stability">
    Expand only when the current waves clear cleanly. A new zone should feel like progress, not like a stress test you cannot afford.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Reinvest after income returns">
    When offline income or a stronger wave payout comes in, use it to patch the weakness that ended your last run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Expansion signal | Expand now? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Base is safe | Yes | Your defense can absorb the new pressure |
| Waves are leaking | No | Fix damage or coverage first |
| Income is climbing | Usually | You can recover after the unlock |
| Last run failed hard | Not yet | Repeat the fix before pushing again |

<Callout type="tip" title="Small Advantage">
If you are unsure whether to upgrade or expand, choose the option that keeps your current defense stable for one more wave cycle.
</Callout>
