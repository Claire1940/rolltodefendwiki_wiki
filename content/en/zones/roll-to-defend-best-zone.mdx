<Section icon={Target} title="When to Buy Your Next Zone">

The right zone timing starts with reading your last wave, not your wallet. If zombies are still slipping through, the next purchase should usually be defense strength first. If the lane is stable, expansion becomes a clean way to push further.

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Rush">
Buying zones too early often creates a fake power spike. You unlock more space, but your defense gets thinner and your income gets locked into recovery.
</Callout>

  <Step num="1" title="Check wave stability">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the bottleneck">
    Decide whether the problem is damage, coverage, roll quality, or income speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Hold backup income">
    Keep enough resources to respond after the unlock. A zone without recovery money is a trap.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Buy the zone">
    Expand only after your current setup can survive a little pressure without breaking.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Rebuild the layout">

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Upgrade vs Zone Spending">

Zone spending works best when you know whether your real problem is power, placement, or economy. A strong zone purchase should follow a stable build, not replace one. That makes your income loop stronger and your rolls more useful.

<Callout type="info" title="Spend in the Right Order">
Early game usually favors survival upgrades first. Zone growth becomes stronger once your current defense can already hold its own.
</Callout>

| Situation | Spend First | Spend Second | Best Reason |
|---|---|---|---|

| Units kill slowly but hold lanes | Damage upgrade | Economy | Faster clears protect your run |
| One path is open | Coverage | Zone | Placement matters more than space |
| Income is healthy | Zone | Roll quality | Growth can scale safely |
| Recent run ended from leaks | Defense fix | Expansion | Solve the repeat failure |
