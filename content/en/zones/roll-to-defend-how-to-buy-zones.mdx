<Section icon={Target} title="Zone Purchase Flow You Can Repeat">

Use the same sequence every time you are deciding whether to buy a zone. This keeps you from expanding too early and helps you turn one stable run into a stronger one. The goal is not to rush the map; it is to make every unlock increase your long-term pace.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the current wave cleanly">
    Wait until your defense is handling zombies without constant panic clicks. If you are still reacting to leaks, the current zone is not ready for expansion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the real bottleneck">
    Decide whether the issue is damage, coverage, unit quality, or timing. Buy zones after the weakest point is already improving, not before.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Keep reserve income available">
    Leave enough currency to recover if the next wave hits harder than expected. A zone purchase should never drain your entire balance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unlock the zone and rebuild fast">
    After the purchase, re-check placement and coverage. New space only helps if your defenders are still pointed at the right path.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Readiness Check | Pass/Fail | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Current waves clear safely | Pass | You are not buying from panic |
| Main lane has backup coverage | Pass | New pressure will not expose a gap |
| You still have reserve income | Pass | You can recover after unlocking |
| One upgrade already solved the last problem | Pass | Expansion is building on stability |
| Zombies still leak every wave | Fail | Fix defense before expanding |

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Rush the Purchase">
Never buy a zone just because the option is available. The menu opening is not a signal by itself; stable defense is the real signal.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="What to Spend Before and After You Buy">

Your money should support the zone, not disappear into it. Before you expand, the best purchase is usually the one that fixes the last failure. After you expand, the best purchase is the one that restores clean wave control and keeps your economy moving forward.

| Spending Priority | Before Zone Buy | After Zone Buy |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Core damage | Rebuild the main lane |
| 2 | Coverage and placement | Add backup coverage |
| 3 | Reserve income | Keep emergency funds ready |
| 4 | Zone purchase | Stabilize the new area |
| 5 | Extra rolls or upgrades | Improve the next cycle |

| Situation | Spend On | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Zombies leak through one lane | Coverage | Buying a bigger map too early |
| Waves survive too long | Damage | Chasing a new zone first |
| Defense is stable | Zone expansion | Hoarding currency forever |
| New zone is unlocked | Reposition units | Leaving the old setup unchanged |

A useful habit is to think in ratios. Keep enough value in defense to survive the next wave, enough reserve to react, and only then turn the rest into progression. That makes every zone unlock feel earned rather than forced.

<Callout type="info" title="Economy Priority">
If your next zone is cheap enough to buy but leaves you unable to survive the following wave, it is still too expensive for your current state.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Common Zone-Buying Mistakes">

A lot of weak runs fail for the same reason: the player buys a zone too soon, then spends the rest of the session repairing the damage from that decision. The fix is usually simple. Slow down, keep a reserve, and let the current defense prove it can carry the next step.

| Mistake | Result | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Buying as soon as income appears | Defense becomes thin | Wait for stable wave clear |
| Spending every coin on expansion | No recovery room | Keep reserve funds |
| Ignoring coverage gaps | Zombies slip through | Reposition before buying |
| Expanding after one good wave | False confidence | Test two or three clean waves |
| Rebuilding too slowly after unlock | New zone stays unstable | Reassign defenders immediately |
