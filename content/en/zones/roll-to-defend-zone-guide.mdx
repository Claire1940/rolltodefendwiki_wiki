<Steps>

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Bottleneck">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Pace Expansion Through Waves">

Zone progress works best when your decisions match the wave rhythm. If the game is pressuring you hard, slow down and reinforce. If the waves are under control, use that breathing room to push forward.

The goal is not to unlock every zone as fast as possible. The goal is to keep each unlock useful. A slower, cleaner path often beats a rushed chain of bad purchases that force you to rebuild twice.

<Callout type="success" title="Cleaner Progression">
A steadier zone climb usually gives you better results because each unlock happens after your defense is ready to benefit from it.
</Callout>

| Wave Situation | Recommended Move | Why It Works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Early waves feel easy | Save and prepare | Builds a safer unlock buffer |
| Mid waves start leaking | Hold the zone | Lets you fix the real problem |
| Waves are stable and fast | Buy the next zone | Converts safety into progress |
| New zone feels harsh | Stop expanding | Prevents a broken resource cycle |

A useful habit is to spend from the top of the bottleneck downward. Start with the issue that made the run feel unstable, then work toward upgrades that improve your next push. This keeps your zone route efficient and prevents waste.

When in doubt, ask one question: does this zone purchase make the next five minutes easier, or does it just make the map bigger? If the answer is only bigger, wait and build first.
